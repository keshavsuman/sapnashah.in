Etiquette Tips

The journey to learn a foreign language is filled with innumerable hurdles. A few motivating tips can help sprint up your endeavour.

Determination and a desire to learn is the key to accelerate your goal. Once these aims are acknowledged, you need to plan the learning process.
‍
Firstly, you must be
1- Attentive
2-Alert
3- Agile

1) Attentive- To be attentive, develop a habit to ‘LISTEN, LISTEN AND LISTEN’. Start listening to as many audios, Ted speakers, watch English movies with subtitles.
Listen to the way the speaker pronounces the words. Pronunciation is quite significant in learning any foreign language.

2) Alert- Attentively be alert while you are watching people speak a foreign language. Watch their body language, which will speak volumes. You can understand and decipher messages while watching a speaker’s body language. Body language emanates facial expressions, tone and moods. Hence, lots could be learnt if this is practiced.

3) Agile- Being agile means being quick, active or lively. Once you start listening attentively and alert while watching, the third step would be to assemble all the thoughts you have learnt from observations actively. Put everything down in your notes and gather all assumptions and knowledge received till now. If you aren't agile, all that is collected will of no use.

The human brain is designed to filter the sound of the words, which help you get familiar with them. It’s essential to familiarize yourself with the terms which are frequently used.
‍
Muscles have memories; that’s why activities like swimming, driving, cycling etc., once learnt, can never be forgotten. The same rule goes for Speaking. Similarly, speaking utilizes muscle coordination to function efficiently and convey your thoughts and ideas verbally. Remember you need not understand every word you hear. Yet, pay attention to what is easy to understand.

TO BE PUT INTO ACTION.
A Quick Recap One must remember to Listen a Lot! Doesn’t matter whether you understand it or not! Listen to the rhythms, pattern sounds, the moods and tone. From body language alone, one can understand a great deal of what is being communicated, so get the meaning first even before reaching the word.

Blending- Try to get at least 15 verbs, pronouns and 15 nouns and 15 adjectives. Focus on the core. With every language, there’s high-frequency content.
‍
Target and aims to learn. Start with your tool kit
‍
Week 1-
What is this all about?
How to say it?
Accept you don’t understand. Repetitive work helps tremendously.

Week 2-3
Collections of Verbs, Pronouns, Nouns and Adjectives can be scattered to help sentence structure
E.g. - you,  me,   they, we,  us Eat,  work, watch, play, drive, give Hot, interesting, happy, challenging,
‍
One can make innumerable sentences enough to express thoughts and feelings. Finally, understand the fact that everything you know is the image inside your mind.

If you speak about the rain, you can smell the petrichor- the earthly scent produced by the rain on the dry soil- you can see the rain falling. These are a few tips if you practice them you can learn a foreign language expeditiously.
