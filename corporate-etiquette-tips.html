Etiquette | Corporate Etiquette Tips and Rules

Etiquette is a large word with innumerable connotations. Life without etiquette is no better than a stray animal. Hence, as humans we are expected to behave in a manner where we are more subtle and more tolerable. It is like a ticket or a label attached to someone for identification. Behaving in a pleasing fashion is like accommodating our words, our appearance, our actions in a way where we may not hurt or harm someone in anyways.

Good etiquette tames your uncaring behavior and makes a more socially acceptable and a pleasing person. Etiquette can be either social or corporate, in this blog I shall concentrate on Corporate Etiquette.

‍
To start with let’s understand our nonverbal communication, which is most subtle and can create an impression without your permission.
‍
• Does the way you walk into a room create an impression?
The answer is ‘Yes’. Your first impression is created with the way enter in a room. The way you walk speaks volumes about your confidence, about your traits, about your nature, your social background. A confident person enters a room wearing a smile, and with an erect posture, whereby the confidence is visible and can give a message to the people on the other side of the table.

Confidence and over confidence have a marginal difference, you must be careful not to cross this thin line. Hence, your first impression is created whenever you meet a new person which can open up doors to your successful career.
‍
• Dress to impress
This is another sensitive issue you must be immensely careful.
Your sense of dressing can communicate about your personality, your background, your choice and much more. It’s a good idea to invest carefully while shopping your office clothes. They have to match up with your designation. At the same time it gives the other person impression how thoughtfully you are and how much importance you give to your job. Hence, dressing up just doesn’t mean to pick up expensive clothes. You must make sure our clothes fit you perfectly.

A misfit expensive outfit can create a most unappealing impression. Secondly, please remember colors have a psychological effect, black is a very authoritative color, your closet must possess this color. Your clothes should be able to pronounce formality and approachability.

Note-Formality means your clothes must demand respect, whereas approachability means your clothes shouldn’t create a bar between you and the people trying to approach you. You can be formal yet approachable depending on your designation.
‍
Note- For women your makeup, your necklines and length of clothes play a vital role depending on the kind of your job, your diligence shouldn’t be replaced with these distracting elements. Your makeup should complement time of the day, the event, the people you are with. For men it is important that you are clean shaved, if you have a beard or moustache it has to be well shaped and trimmed. Unkempt people can create a displeasing impression.

‍
Along with your clothes you also need to give importance to your accessories, Like your tie, your jewelry its essential to be elegant with your accessories. Another area you must not neglect is your feet, your feet must be neat and clean; for women your toenails must be well kept, polished and should look attractive.

‍
Note-Avoid chipped off nail-polish on your hands as well as your toes.

‍
Am I supposed to give a handshake to men and women?
This is a very formal act you must be very careful with the culture, country and their beliefs in this delicate issue. Mostly, a handshake isn’t gender bar, however if the company policy or country and culture of the place follow a conservative policy you must be careful. Nevertheless, a handshake is to be considered to be a warm welcome. Also, wait for your senior to stretch his/her hand out for a shake.

Be careful your handshake is not a loose and unwelcoming at the same time it shouldn’t be that your handshake prolongs and becomes awkward for the other person. So in a nutshell handshake should be warm , firm and respectful.

‍
After a handshake should I wait for the person to ask me to sit or it is to be considered an implicit action?
Does this act of sitting portray etiquette. Absolutely, it is surely an implicit act to sit after a handshake, however it is always polite to ask the host before you sit. Your asking could be just a humble gesture, but please do ask. Another significant point is do not be clumsy or be in a rush to pull the chair and sit, it will spoil all your previous efforts. Be a little stylish and gentle while pulling the chair, placing your papers or files. It’s essential to ask before placing your belongings on the table. Your files and bags shouldn’t fill-up the table making it look cluttered and untidy.

‍
These are some significant etiquette tips on nonverbal communication skills. You may leave your comment below and can approach me for the workshops where all these skills will be sharpened and chiseled where you can present yourself in a more pleasing fashion.
